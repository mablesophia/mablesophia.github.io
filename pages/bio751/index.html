You are not to modify the "body" in anyway, only adjust "L" and "W". Also, make sure you put the paperclip at the base of your helicopter. A mixture of research on flight dynamics as well as empirical observation must be summarized into a report, answering the questions below. Although the work is done in pairs, each student must write and submit his/her own report. You are allowed NO MORE than 20 helicopters, and no more than 200 flights TOTAL. For example, you could make 20 helicopters and fly each of them 10 times, recording their L and W and flight time. Flight times should be recorded to the nearest hundredth of a second. Flights are supposed to be drops from a height of 9 feet measured from the ground to the bottom of the copter when being held suspended (preflight). It also may behoove you to annotate your numeric records, i.e., in case a copter hits a wall or gets blown out of the sky by the Apaches or Blackwater.

      Good luck.

  • Due 10/5 (Tuesday) before 5:00PM:

    1. Given a sample (x1, ..., xn) from a Normal distribution with unknown mean μ and known variance σ2, what is the maximum likelihood estimate of μ?
    2. Learn about multivariate linear regression.
      • Study the linear algebra notation used in linear regression. A useful text is Seber and Lee, Chapter 3.
      • For the model we defined in class, find the Least Square Estimates (LSE) of β in matrix notation. Show that the LSE is unbiased.
      • Let εhat = Y-Xβ. Find E(εhat), Var(εhat), and show that E{(εhat)T(εhat)} = (n-p) σ2.
      • Download the data on babies' health. Fit a linear model of birthweight on an intercept, mother's age, and mother's height using the R command lm(). Verify the coefficients using the LSE of β in matrix notation.
    3. The Surgeon General says that "Smoking by pregnant women may result in fetal injury, premature birth, and low birthweight." Use the data on babies' health to prepare a short (less than 2 pages) report supporting or opposing the opinion that smoking by pregnant women can cause low birth weights. Notes and hints:
      • You can stick with using the R command lm() for the models you construct here.
      • Write a short introduction discussing the biology and examples of previous studies.
      • Maternal smoking status might be confounded with other variables that might cause low birthweight. The data include various covariates. Assess the relation of these variabes with birth weight and smoking status.
      • If you find smoking has an effect on birthweights, is it the same for different values of the other covariates? e.g. is the effect the same for tall women and short women?
      • Estimate the effect of smoking on birth weight after correcting for any other variable that also appears to cause low birth weight.
      • Which of the covariates has the biggest effect on birth weight?

    1. From the last homework: Compare 95% confidence intervals for the mean constructed using the CLT with 95% confidence intervals constructed using the bootstrap.         Hint 2: You may also refer to Professor Rohde's notes on this subject; however, please do not look at Rohde's notes until after trying this problem on your own for 30 min.
